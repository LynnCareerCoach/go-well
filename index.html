THE PROBLEM TO SOLVE

What is the difference between world maps using different CRS?

Goode Homosoline: the sizes of each continent are accurate in proportion to the others, but distances are distorted especially near the poles

Equidistant Cylindrical: Navigation is easier (north and south stay constant) but latitudes are equally spaced, giving more accurate representation of distance along the north-south axis

Mercator: Better for navigation (north and south stay constant like equidistant cylindrical), but greater distortion at the poles makes continents farther from the Equator seem bigger than they actually are

Robinson: Less distortion in the middle of the map as compared to Mercator, and the poles have more severely curved lines for longitude, so the distances between continents can be grasped more accurately
